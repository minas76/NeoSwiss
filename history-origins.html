Historical context

Origins & the Swiss masters

The International Typographic Style established rigorous grid and typographic methods. Its clarity and function-first approach continue to inform contemporary practice.

Key figures

  • Josef Müller‑Brockmann — formalized grid theory and taught modular systems.
  • Max Bill — rationalist approach to visual form.
  • Armin Hofmann — balance and typographic clarity.

Principles established

Objective typography, rigorous alignment, and an emphasis on functional clarity formed the backbone of mid‑century Swiss Design.

Legacy

These principles created enduring systems that designers and engineers now interpret for screens and systems.
